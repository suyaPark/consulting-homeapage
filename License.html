BUSINESS LICENSE

From incorporation to marketing & HR, P&D can help you with everything you need to enter the market for the first time. Specifically:
1. Registering as an individual business household:

This option involves registering as a self-employed individual.

2. Registering to establish an enterprise:

Unlike registering as an individual business household, starting a business by establishing an enterprise provides entrepreneurs with several options under the Law on Enterprises 2020, effective from January 1, 2021, as follows:
- Establishing a sole proprietorship.
- Establishing a limited liability company (LLC):
- Single-member LLC.
- Multi-member LLC.
- Establishing a joint-stock company.
- Establishing a partnership (for certain specific fields).

To help entrepreneurs better understand the nature of these business forms as well as their advantages and disadvantages before choosing to register your business activities, Park & Duong Consulting provides the following analysis of the implementation options for business registration:
Registering a business as an individual business household
Based on legal grounds Decree No. 01/2021/Nƒê-CP.
Definition An individual or members of a household register and take full responsibility for the business activities of the business household using their own assets. In the case of a business household registered by members of a household, one member is authorized to act as the representative of the business household. The individual who registers the business household and is authorized by the members of the household to act as the representative is the head of the business household. A business household is only allowed to register one business household nationwide and has the right to contribute capital, purchase shares, and acquire capital contributions in enterprises as an individual.
Advantage There is no requirement to submit monthly tax declarations. The business household applies a lump-sum tax regime and issues direct invoices, which simplifies the bookkeeping and accounting records for the business owner.
The small scale of the business is suitable for household businesses or individuals starting their business, especially those directly supplying consumer goods to individuals, such as clothing trading, restaurant services, food and beverage, fashion design, personal consumer goods trading, and grocery stores. Therefore, the main customers are individuals rather than businesses.
Businesses registered as individual business households have the option to convert to the establishment of an enterprise, either a limited liability company (LLC) or a joint-stock company.
The management and administration of business operations are kept simple.
Disadvantage Because it is a direct tax, when business entities, as customers or trading partners, purchase goods from an individual business household, they are not entitled to deduct value-added tax (VAT). Therefore, many businesses may not prefer to buy goods or services from individual business households.
According to legal regulations, an individual business household does not have legal personality, which means it has unlimited liability for its business operations. The business owner is personally liable for the civil liabilities of the business household. This is a significant limitation of individual business households compared to limited liability companies (LLCs) and joint-stock companies. In these types of enterprises, the contributors or shareholders have limited liability within the scope of their contributed capital in the business operations.

Members can be organizations or individuals, with a minimum of two members and a maximum of fifty.
The company obtains legal entity status upon receiving the Certificate of Business Registration.		 The capital is divided into shares.
Shareholders have limited liability based on their contributed capital.
Shareholders can freely transfer their shares.
The company has legal personality upon obtaining the Business Registration Certificate.
The company has the right to issue securities to the public.
Advantage As the sole owner, a private enterprise has full control over business decisions.
The owner's unlimited liability fosters trust and offers flexibility compared to other types of businesses.		 Combines the reputations of multiple individuals.

Capital transfer within the company is relatively easy, attracting a wide range of participants, including civil servants.
